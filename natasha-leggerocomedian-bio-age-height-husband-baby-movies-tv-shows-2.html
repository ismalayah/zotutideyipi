Natasha Leggero Biography

The Rockford, Illinois native Natasha Leggero is a well-known actress, comedian, and writer. Leggero rose to fame after appearing as the host of MTV’s The 70s House in 2005 and for her tenure as a regular roundtable panelist on Chelsea Handler’s late-night talk show Chelsea Lately. Leggero’s comedy, characterized as observational, has been noted by critics for its use of satire as a commentary on such themes as celebrity culture and class.

Her stand-up television appearances include performances on The Tonight Show with Jay Leno, Last Call with Carson Daly, The Late Late Show with Craig Ferguson, The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon and she has appeared on multiple Comedy Central stand-up programs as well as their animated show Ugly Americans, wherein Leggero voiced Callie Maggotbone. Leggero also appeared in season 5, episode 7 of The League, as Andre’s (Paul Scheer) comedy class teacher. She co-created the period sitcom Another Period for Comedy Central in 2015, in which she starred together with co-creator Riki Lindhome.

Natasha Leggero Education

Leggero began performing when she was 10 years old in plays in Chicago. She attended Rockford East High School and worked at a grocery store as a teenager. Leggero attended Illinois State University after graduating from high school, where she studied for two years and spent a semester studying abroad in England. Leggero auditioned for the Stella Adler Conservatory in Chicago while at Illinois State and was accepted into the theater program.

Leggero then relocated to New York City to attend the conservatory, where she spend two years studying. She moved to Sydney in 1996, Australia with her then-boyfriend and lived there for a year before returning to New York to attend Hunter College, where she graduated in 2000 with a B.A. in Theater criticism. Shortly before the September 11 attacks, Leggero relocated to Los Angeles.

Natasha Leggero Career

Natasha Leggero shot an unaired pilot called The Strip for NBC, with Ben Garant and Tom Lennon from Reno 911!. Leggero was a judge alongside Andy Kindler and Greg Giraldo on the 2010 season of NBC’s Last Comic Standing, hosted by Craig Robinson. Leggero portrayed the character of Emma in NBC’s 2011 sitcom Free Agents. Leggero co-hosted The Lavender Hour podcast from 2010 to January 2012, with comedian Duncan Trussell.


Natasha Leggero Photo

From 2010 to 2012, Leggero voiced the role of Callie Maggotbone on the animated series Ugly Americans. In 2012, Leggero also appeared in six episodes of NBC’s Are You There, Chelsea? as Nikki, who was Rick’s ex-girlfriend. She was featured in every first-season episode starting with the second episode, “Sloane’s Ex.”

In the web series Burning Love, she also played the sex-obsessed Haley, a spoof of the TV series The Bachelor and The Bachelorette. Leggero appeared in an episode of the Drunk History television series on Comedy Central in 2013. Leggero participated in the Comedy Central Roast of James Franco on September 2, 2013, as one of the Roasters; the following day, Brickleberry premiered in its second season. She voiced the busty ranger Ethel in place of Kaitlin Olson, who was in the first season only.

Tubbin’ with Tash debuted on 2nd October 2013 on Leggero’s YouTube channel. The premise of the show is Leggero interviewing guests while in a hot tub. She debuted her Comedy Central standup special in August 2015, Live at Bimbo’s (originally entitled Diamond Pussy), which she filmed in San Francisco.

Leggero and Riki Lindhome created the Comedy Central series Another Period in 2015, which they also starred in together; the series premiered June 23, 2015, and was renewed for a second season, which premiered June 15, 2016. It was renewed for a third season on May 23, 2016, which aired in 2018. Leggero and husband Moshe Kasher embarked on The Honeymoon Tour in 2016 and 2017, in which they performed standup shows and provided humorous relationship advice throughout the United States.

New Year’s Eve incident

Leggero was criticized on December 31, 2013, for a joke she made during NBC’s New Year’s Eve with Carson Daly, when, in response to a controversial tweet posted by SpaghettiOs on the anniversary of the Pearl Harbor attack, she quipped: “It sucks that the only survivors of Pearl Harbor are being mocked by the only food they can still chew.”

Leggero’s remarks were met with a backlash over social media; in response, Leggero posted in a blog entry that she did not feel that “the amazing courage of American veterans and specifically those who survived Pearl Harbor is in any way diminished by a comedian making a joke about dentures on television.

Do we really believe that the people who fought and defended our freedom against Nazis and the Axis powers will find a joke about Spaghetti O’s too much to bear? Sorry, I have more respect for Veterans than to think their honor can be impugned by a glamorous, charming comedian in a fur hat.” Leggero also called on those offended by the remarks to donate to Disabled American Veterans.
